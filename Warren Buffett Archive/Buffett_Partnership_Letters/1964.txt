1964 Partnership Letters
------------------------
**January 18, 1964 – Our Performance in 1963.**  Buffett reported that the partnership achieved a 38.7 % gain in 1963 versus the Dow’s 20.7 % rise, resulting in a margin of eighteen percentage points【86167187012945†L0-L65】.  He provided a table summarising results from 1957‑1963 and reiterated that performance should be measured relative to the Dow.  Buffett cautioned partners not to expect a consistent eighteen‑point advantage and emphasised that periods of underperformance were inevitable.

**July 8, 1964 – First Half Performance.**  In the July letter Buffett noted that the partnership’s first‑half results were roughly in line with the Dow, which rose about 10 %【771858684053792†L0-L119】.  He explained that the portfolio continued to consist of undervalued securities and control situations and that he maintained a conservative stance because attractive opportunities were scarce.  Buffett reiterated that the partnership would not chase popular stocks and would concentrate on areas where he believed value existed【771858684053792†L0-L119】.
