1965 Partnership Letters
------------------------
**January 18, 1965 – Our Performance in 1964.**  Buffett’s January 1965 letter showed that the partnership gained about 20 % in 1964 compared with the Dow’s 14 % return【293022810048930†L0-L126】.  He remarked that beating the index in strong bull markets was particularly difficult and that partners should expect to lag the Dow during some periods.  Buffett discussed the composition of the portfolio, noting that “generals” and “control” situations continued to be the main sources of profit.

**July 9, 1965 – First Half Performance.**  Buffett reported that the partnership’s results in the first half of 1965 exceeded the Dow by only a few points and cautioned that performance could vary widely from year to year【299248197880616†L0-L142】.  He described progress on acquiring controlling interests in companies and stressed the importance of investing only where he understood the business and felt comfortable with the management.

**November 1, 1965 – Administrative.**  This short letter asked partners to return commitment letters for 1966 and provided valuation information for Berkshire Hathaway stock.  Buffett reiterated the partnership’s ground rules and explained that partners who could not tolerate periodic market declines should not participate【88970254187374†L4-L53】.
